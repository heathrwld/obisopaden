Lechon Recipe

Lechon is a traditional Filipino dish that features a whole roasted pig, known for its crispy skin and tender, flavorful meat. It is a centerpiece during special occasions and celebrations. Marinated with a blend of spices, lemongrass, and garlic, this dish is especially famous in Cebu, where it is considered the best in the Philippines.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole pig (20 kg)
  • 10-20 bundles of lemongrass
  • 15 bay leaves
  • 1 kg garlic
  • 1 kg (2.2 lb) onion
  • 2-3 cups salt and pepper

Cooking Steps:

  1. Wash the pig inside and out, bathe it with hot water, and remove any hairs using a blade.
  2. Rub the mixture of salt and pepper on the pig’s skin and inside cavity. Place the seasonings, ingredients, and spices inside the pig and stitch it closed.
  3. Skewer the pig onto a long bamboo stick.
  4. Roast the pig over hot charcoal for 4 to 5 hours, until the meat is tender.
  5. To achieve a red and crispy skin, brush the pig with soy sauce during roasting.
