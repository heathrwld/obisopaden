Humba Recipe

Humba is a traditional Filipino dish popular in the Visayas and Mindanao regions. It features pork belly simmered until tender in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, brown sugar, and pineapple juice, with additional flavors from tomato sauce or Sprite for a unique taste. This creates a sweet and savory sauce that pairs perfectly with steamed rice, making it a favorite for family gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 1.2kg Pork Belly
  • 6 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1.5 teaspoon Black Pepper
  • 2 tbsp Sugar
  • 220 ml Pineapple Juice
  • 3 tbsp Oil
  • 2 Onions
  • 6 cloves Garlic
  • 3 tbsp Vinegar
  • 2 pcs Bayleaf
  • 100 grams Black Beans
  • 50 grams Banana Blossom

Cooking Steps:

  1. Marinate the pork belly with soy sauce, black pepper, sugar, and pineapple juice for at least 20 minutes.
  2. Sauté onions and garlic in a large pan until softened.
  3. Add the marinated pork belly and cook on high heat until lightly brown.
  4. Add vinegar and bayleaf, and cook on high heat. Add water if the pork becomes dry, then cook on low heat until soft.
  5. Add black beans and banana blossom, and cook until everything is tender.
