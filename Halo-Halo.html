Halo-Halo Recipe

Halo-halo, or "mix-mix" in English, is a popular Filipino cold dessert made up of shaved ice, evaporated milk or coconut milk, and various sweet ingredients layered together in a tall glass. It is perfect in the Philippines because the climate is hot, and it is one of the best-selling cold desserts in the country.

Ingredients:

  • 1-2 tablespoons syrup-packed red mung beans, white beans, and/or garbanzo beans
  • 1 tablespoon sweetened jackfruit
  • 1 tablespoon macapuno strings (coconut sport)
  • 1 tablespoon nata de coconut (coconut gel)
  • 1 cup shaved ice
  • 1 tablespoon ube halaya jam
  • 2 tablespoons leche flan, purchased
  • 2 to 4 tablespoons evaporated milk
  • 2 tablespoons ube ice cream

Cooking Steps:

  1. In a tall glass or a trifle dish, layer the assorted beans, sweetened jackfruit, coconut gel, and coconut strings. Feel free to add more or less of any of the ingredients you enjoy.
  2. Add shaved ice to fill the glass, leaving room for toppings. Top with ube halaya, leche flan, and ube ice cream.
  3. To finish the halo-halo recipe, drizzle evaporated milk over the top. Mix and enjoy. Halo-halo is best enjoyed right away.
